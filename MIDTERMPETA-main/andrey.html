How does Cholesterol and Cell Membrane Works?
What is Cholesterol?

Cholesterol is a crucial component of animal cell membranes. It is embedded between the phospholipids in the lipid bilayer and plays several essential roles that help maintain membrane structure and function.

What does Cholestrols' prevent?

Cholesterol prevents the membrane from becoming too rigid in cold temperatures and too fluid in high temperatures.

What Does Cholesterol's Add?

Cholesterol adds strength and stability to the membrane by fitting snugly between phospholipids, preventing them from moving too freely.
