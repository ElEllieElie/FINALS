What is the Phospholipid Bilayer?

The Phospholipid Bilayer is a thin layer of phospholipid molecules that forms the fundamental structures of the plasma membrane, acting as a barrier to separate the inside of a cell from its outside environment. It consists of two layers of phospholipids, with hydrophilic heads and hydrophobic tails. This arrangement is crucial because it allows selective permeability and fluidity.
