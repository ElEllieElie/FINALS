Membrane Protein? What is it?

Membrane proteins are proteins that are either part of or interact with the biological membranes of cells and organelles. They make up about a third of all proteins in living organisms and are essential for a wide range of cellular processes. They are classified based on their association with the membrane. The 3 classifications are:

Integral/Intrinsic Membrane Proteins. They are permanently embedded in the membrane. They often spanning the entire lipid bilayer.

Peripheral/Extrinsic Membrane Proteins. They are temporarily attached to the membrane surface or to integral proteins, and are easily removable.

Lipid-Anchored Proteins. Covalently attached to lipids within the membrane, which anchors them to the membrane.
